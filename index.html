~ OYAKODON ~

Oyakodon is a classic Japanese dish that consists of chicken and onions simmered in a sweet and savory sauce, served over a bowl of steamed rice and topped with a soft-cooked egg. It's a delicious and comforting meal that's relatively easy to prepare.
Here's a basic recipe for Oyakodon:



INGREDIENTS:

  • For the Chicken and Onion Mixture:
    • 2 boneless, skinless chicken thighs (or 1 chicken breast), thinly sliced
    • 1 onion, thinly sliced
    • 2-3 green onions, chopped (for garnish)
    • 2 cups cooked Japanese short-grain rice (sushi rice)

  • For the Oyakodon Sauce:
    • 2/3 cup dashi stock (you can use instant dashi or make your own by combining bonito flakes and kombu seaweed)
    • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 2 tablespoons mirin (sweet rice wine)
    • 1 tablespoon sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon sake (optional)
    • 2-3 large eggs


Steps:


  1. Prepare the Dashi Stock: If you're making your own dashi stock, combine 2/3 cup of water with a small piece of kombu seaweed in a saucepan. Heat it over medium heat until small bubbles form around the edges (do not boil). Remove the kombu seaweed, add a handful of bonito flakes, and simmer for a few minutes. Strain the liquid to obtain dashi stock.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and sake (if using). Add the dashi stock to this mixture and set it aside.
  3. Cook the Chicken and Onions: In a large, shallow pan or skillet, arrange the sliced onions evenly on the bottom. Place the chicken slices on top of the onions. Pour the sauce mixture over the chicken and onions.
  4. Simmer: Heat the pan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cover the pan and let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through, and the onions have softened.
  5. Add Eggs: Gently beat the eggs in a bowl. Once the chicken is cooked, drizzle the beaten eggs evenly over the chicken and onions. Cover the pan again and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the eggs are just set but still slightly runny.
  6. Serve: Place a portion of cooked rice in each serving bowl. Using a spatula, carefully scoop the chicken, onions, and eggs from the pan and place them on top of the rice.
  7. Garnish: Sprinkle chopped green onions over the top for added flavor and color.
  8. Serve hot: Oyakodon is traditionally served hot and is ready to enjoy!
